# Aggressive Creative Style: Image & Video Ad Templates

This file covers the **Aggressive Creative Style** — ad creative adapted from the Platform-Hosted direct response page format used on Nestuge and Selar.

The aggressive style is the default for Nigerian audiences, MIA Academy products, and any campaign targeting people in Nigeria or the Nigerian diaspora (Lagos, Abuja, London, Houston, Toronto, Dubai). It's also applicable for any digital product campaign where the audience pain is financial, career-related, or built around watching peers win while they fall behind.

**This style is not appropriate for:**
- B2B SaaS targeting enterprise decision-makers
- Professional services where tone must remain formal
- Audiences where the pain is primarily functional, not emotional

---

## What the Aggressive Style Is

The aggressive creative style mirrors how the most effective Nigerian digital product sellers write their Nestuge and Selar pages:

| Element | Standard Style | Aggressive Style |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| Hook | Financial data, benefit claim | Competitor comparison, present-tense jealousy or fear |
| Tone | Authoritative, rational | Emotionally urgent, direct, slightly confrontational |
| Language | Brand voice | Audience's own Twitter/X or LinkedIn language |
| Pain framing | Named problem | Named pain + named consequence right now |
| Proof | Testimonials with results | Raw DM/chat screenshots OR testimonials with before/after specifics |
| Urgency | Deadline or scarcity | Framed as cost of inaction — "every day you wait" |
| Visual style | Polished, branded | High-contrast, bold text, feels raw and direct |
| CTA energy | Inviting ("Start free") | Closing ("Stop watching. Start building.") |

---

## Before Using This Style

Ask the user if they have not specified:

> "For Nigerian/diaspora audiences and digital product campaigns, I default to the aggressive creative style — competitor comparison hooks, emotional urgency, and raw proof framing. Do you want aggressive style or standard style for this campaign?"

**Default to aggressive when:**
- Target market is Nigeria, Nigerian diaspora, or West Africa
- Product is a course, bootcamp, challenge, handbook, masterclass, or template library
- Audience pain is financial (underpaid, missing USD opportunities, watching peers advance)
- User mentions MIA Academy, Selar, Nestuge, or any Nigerian digital product creator

**Default to standard when:**
- B2B SaaS, enterprise, or formal professional audience
- Target market is US/UK without specific Nigerian/diaspora context
- User explicitly requests standard or brand-safe tone

## Aggressive Creative: Core Patterns

### Hook Patterns

| Hook Type | Formula | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Competitor Comparison | "Your competitors are [winning]. You're still [struggling]." | "Your mates are signing USD contracts. You're still on ₦180K/month." |
| Financial Tension | "[Peers] are making [₦/$ figure]. Here's what's different." | "She makes $3,000/month from Lagos. Same market. Same phone. Different skill." |
| Present-Tense Loss | "Right now, while you're [doing X], someone just [won Y]." | "Right now, a marketer in Abuja just got a $2,500/month job offer. No japa required." |
| Identity Challenge | "You know you're more than [current label]." | "Your oga calls you 'social media person.' Your strategy is worth more than that." |
| Exact Number | "₦[X]/month. That's what [skill] earns right now." | "₦350,000/month doing the same thing you already do — with the right title." |

### Pain-Vision-CTA Arc (Aggressive)

```
Hook: [Competitor is winning / peer is earning / you're being left behind]
Pain: Name the specific daily reality they hate (present tense)
Gap: "They know something you don't: [one insight]"
Vision: What their life looks like on the other side
Proof: One concrete result (₦ figure, USD contract, promotion)
CTA: Closing, not inviting. "Apply now." not "Learn more."
```

### Emotional Sequence (for 30-60s video)

```
0-3s:   HOOK — Financial comparison or present-tense loss
3-10s:  PAIN — Name the daily reality they hate
10-20s: GAP — "Here's what's different about those who are winning"
20-35s: VISION — Specific outcomes with ₦/$ figures
35-50s: PROOF — One before/after result or screenshot reference
50-60s: CTA — Hard close. Not soft.
```

### Image Ad Brief: The USD Contract Gap

**Creative Brief**
- Creative type: Financial Tension
- Creative style: Aggressive
- Funnel stage: TOFU
- Platform: Meta (Facebook & Instagram)
- Placement: Feed, Stories & Reels
- Dimensions: 1080x1080 (1:1) & 1080x1920 (9:16)

**Concept**
- Angle: Financial Tension — ₦200K/month vs. $3,000/month USD contrast
- Core message: Same skill, different packaging. The gap is what they know how to prove.
- Emotional tone: Recognition + aspiration + mild urgency
- Audience context: Nigerian marketers who see peers landing USD remote contracts on LinkedIn and Twitter/X, feeling stuck on local salaries while knowing they have the skills to compete internationally

**On-Image Copy**
- Headline: "₦200K/month. Or $3,000/month. Same skill. Different packaging."
- Supporting text: "Most Nigerian marketers are 12 weeks away from the second number."
- CTA text: Take the Quiz

**Platform Ad Copy**
- Primary text: "Your colleague just signed a $2,800/month remote contract. Product Marketing Manager at a US SaaS company. You have more experience. The difference isn't skill. It's proof. 12 weeks. 12 deliverables. One program that builds the portfolio that gets you the interview."
- Headline: "Same Skill. Triple the Income."
- Description: "Apply before Jun 8 cohort fills."

**Visual Direction**
- Primary visual: Split-composition — left side shows ₦200,000 local salary figure in muted tones, right side shows $3,000/month figure in bold. A Nigerian marketer in the center looking toward the right.
- Composition: Text-dominant. Figures are large. Person is secondary to the numbers.
- Color palette / mood: Deep navy + high-contrast white text. Feels direct, financial, not aspirational lifestyle.
- Typography direction: Bold sans-serif (Inter/DM Sans), heavy weight, 2-column layout for the ₦ vs $ contrast

**Elements**
- Background: Dark solid or subtle gradient — navy, charcoal
- Foreground subject: Large ₦ and $ figures as the primary visual anchor
- Text placement: Headline across the center third of the image
- Logo placement: MIA Academy wordmark, bottom-left, small
- Overlays: None — let the numbers do the work

**Production Notes**
- Brand constraints: MIA Academy brand colors acceptable but not mandatory — contrast matters more than brand consistency here
- What to avoid: Stock photos of smiling people at laptops, generic "success" imagery, any visual that looks like a corporate HR ad
- Variations needed: Feed 1:1 and Stories/Reels 9:16

### Image Ad Brief: The Nigerian CV Filter

**Creative Brief**
- Creative type: Present-Tense Loss
- Creative style: Aggressive
- Funnel stage: TOFU
- Platform: Meta (Facebook & Instagram)
- Placement: Feed, Stories & Reels
- Dimensions: 1080x1080 (1:1) & 1080x1920 (9:16)

**Concept**
- Angle: Present-Tense Loss — the cost of not having a portfolio right now
- Core message: Right now, someone with less experience than you just got shortlisted. Portfolio was the difference.
- Emotional tone: Urgency + fear of inaction + clear path forward
- Audience context: Nigerian marketers applying to international roles and being filtered out without feedback, knowing they have skills but can't prove them

**On-Image Copy**
- Headline: "Right now, a marketer in Abuja just got shortlisted for a $2,500/month role."
- Supporting text: "No japa. No connection. Just a portfolio that showed what they could build."
- CTA text: Build Yours

**Platform Ad Copy**
- Primary text: "91% of marketers say they use AI. Only 23% can prove it. The ones who can are the ones getting shortlisted. 12 portfolio-ready deliverables. 13 weeks. MIA Academy."
- Headline: "They Had a Portfolio. You Don't. Yet."
- Description: "Next cohort: Jun 8. Limited seats."

**Visual Direction**
- Primary visual: Text-dominant image. The headline IS the image.
- Composition: Large bold headline text centered or left-aligned, white on dark. No person needed — the copy carries the weight.
- Color palette / mood: High contrast — white text on deep navy or dark green. Feels like an alert, not an ad.
- Typography direction: Large, bold, urgent. Headline should fill 60-70% of the image space.

**Elements**
- Background: Solid dark — navy, charcoal, or dark green
- Foreground subject: Typography-only
- Text placement: Headline center, supporting text below
- Logo placement: MIA Academy, bottom-right, small
- Overlays: A subtle notification icon (🔔 or 🚨) above the headline — optional

**Production Notes**
- Brand constraints: Dark background required — this needs to feel like a disruption in the feed
- What to avoid: Bright lifestyle colors, stock photography, anything that looks cheerful or safe
- Variations needed: Feed 1:1 and Stories/Reels 9:16

### Video Script Brief: Your Colleagues Are Getting the Interviews

**Creative Brief**
- Creative type: Competitor Comparison
- Creative style: Aggressive
- Funnel stage: TOFU
- Platform: Meta (Reels) + TikTok
- Duration: 45s
- Aspect ratio: 9:16

**Concept**
- Video idea: Creator speaks directly to camera addressing the gap between Nigerian marketers who have skills but no portfolio and the ones getting $2,000–$3,000/month USD roles. Closes with Two Choices frame.
- Core angle: Same market, same skills, different result — the difference is proof, not ability
- One-line promise: You'll understand exactly why you're not getting the interviews and what to do about it in 13 weeks
- Audience context: Nigerian marketers watching peers post LinkedIn updates about USD contracts, applying to international roles and never hearing back, frustrated that skill isn't translating to opportunity

**Hook (0:00 – 0:03)**
- Hook type: Competitor Comparison
- Hook line: "Your colleagues are landing $3,000/month remote roles. You're still waiting to hear back."
- Visual: Creator on camera, direct address, confident, slightly confrontational
- Text overlay: "Your colleagues are landing $3K/month roles." (large text, high contrast)
- Audio: Voice direct — no warmup, no intro

**Body**

| Timestamp | Script / VO | On-Screen Visual | Text Overlay | Sound |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|--------------|-------|
| 0:03–0:10 | "You apply. You don't hear back. Not because you don't have the skill. Because they can't see the skill. They see a CV. Not a portfolio." | Creator on camera | "They see a CV. Not a portfolio." | Minimal background music, building |
| 0:10–0:20 | "The marketers getting these roles — they're walking in with GTM plans, positioning docs, lifecycle campaigns they actually built. Not listed on a CV. Shown." | Cut to text card showing list: GTM plan / Positioning doc / Lifecycle campaign | "12 deliverables. Built and ready to show." | Music builds slightly |
| 0:20–0:30 | "Product and Growth Marketing with AI for Modern Marketers. 13 weeks. You leave with 12 completed deliverables, 50 templates, and a portfolio that can compete internationally. No japa required." | Product name on screen / brief visual of materials | "13 weeks. 12 deliverables. International-ready." | Music peaks |
| 0:30–0:40 | "Theodore landed a role paying 40% more. Tunde signed a USD contract with a US SaaS company. Chidinma got promoted to lead GTM. They showed their work. That's it." | Text testimonials rapid-fire — name, result, one line each | Result captions: "40% raise" / "USD contract" / "GTM lead" | Music steady |

- CTA line: "You have two choices. Keep applying and not hearing back. Or spend 13 weeks building the portfolio that changes that. Next cohort: June 8. Link in bio."

- Text overlay: "Next cohort: June 8 — Link in bio"

- Music / mood: Tense, building — not inspirational. Think documentary, not motivational speaker. If music distracts from the urgency, cut it entirely and let the voice carry.
- Pacing: Fast cuts. No scene should hold for more than 4 seconds without a text overlay moving. Silence is not allowed.
- Brand constraints: MIA Academy — dark backgrounds acceptable. Do NOT use the program name as text in a cheerful way. State it plainly.
- What to avoid: Smiling opener, slow intro, lifestyle b-roll, soft CTA ("learn more," "check it out"), upbeat music that undercuts the urgency
- Talent / presenter: James Praise on camera if possible. Voiceover with text-only works as a backup.

## Aggressive Style: What Makes These Work

### On Image Ads

- **The headline is the visual.** In aggressive image creative, the headline often IS the primary visual element. Not a hero image with a small headline — a large bold statement that punches.
- **Numbers do more work than claims.** "₦200K/month vs. $3,000/month" stops the scroll. "Improve your career" does not.
- **Dark backgrounds signal urgency.** Bright, polished imagery signals aspiration. Dark, high-contrast imagery signals consequence, financial seriousness, and urgency. Match the tone.
- **The supporting text amplifies, doesn't explain.** "Most Nigerian marketers are 12 weeks away from the second number" doesn't explain — it sharpens the hook without defusing it.

### On Video Ads

- **Start with the emotional pain, not the product.** Never open with your brand name, credentials, or "In this video, I'll show you." Open with the gut punch.
- **The Two Choices close converts.** Laying out Choice 1 (stay the same) vs. Choice 2 (take action) is a proven closing frame for video. Use it in the last 5-8 seconds.
- **Rapid-fire testimonials with numbers beat long testimonials.** "40% raise. USD contract. GTM promotion." Three results in 8 seconds outperforms one testimonial told as a story for 20 seconds.
- **Silence is not allowed.** Every second of a aggressive-style video must be earning attention. No fade-in, no intro, no breathing room.

### Cultural Context (Nigeria + Diaspora)

- **"No japa required"** is a specific resonance hook. It acknowledges the common belief that you need to leave Nigeria to earn in dollars and directly contradicts it. Use when the product enables international income without emigrating.
- **₦/$ contrast is the primary financial frame.** Not annual US salary data. Not median global rates. The Naira-to-dollar transition is the emotional axis of this audience's aspiration.
- **"Your oga"** is a specific cultural reference to a superior or boss. Using it correctly signals cultural fluency. Use sparingly and accurately.
- **LinkedIn and Twitter/X language register.** The audience is digitally native, professionally ambitious, and hyper-aware of their peers' wins. The copy should sound like something they'd screenshot and share. If it sounds like an HR department wrote it, it's wrong.

## Pairing with the Direct Response Sales Page

Aggressive ad creatives are designed to land on platform-hosted (Nestuge/Selar) sales pages using the same emotional register. The ad-to-page flow should be:

| Ad Element | Page Element |
|-----------|-------------|
| Competitor comparison hook | Competitor comparison hook (same emotional entry) |
| ₦/$ financial tension | Amplified in the hook block |
| "12 deliverables" proof | Expanded in "What You'll Get" section |
| Rapid-fire testimonials | Expanded in testimonials section |
| Two Choices close (video) | Two Choices close on page |
| "Link in bio / Learn more" CTA | Application form or Selar checkout |

**Message match is critical.** The ad hook must match the page hook in emotional register. If the ad is aggressive and the page is soft, the conversion drops.
