# Outbound Email Frameworks & Templates

**Master Reference for Clay-Powered Outbound Sequences**

This reference covers copywriting frameworks, sequence archetypes by business objective, hook patterns, CTA variations, and subject line formulas. Use this as the primary reference when building outbound email sequences in Clay. The companion files (3 Email Sequence, 4 Email Sequence) provide detailed examples of two specific archetypes.

---

## Part 1: Cold Email Copywriting Frameworks

These frameworks define the structural logic of each email. Choose the framework that matches your signal strength and buyer awareness level.

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

Best for: Prospects who recognize the problem but haven't prioritized solving it.

**Structure:**
1. Name the problem (tied to enrichment signal)
2. Agitate by describing consequences of inaction
3. Present your solution as the resolution

**Template:**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

{{job_title}}s at companies like {{company_name}} often tell us {{pain_statement}}.

Left unchecked, this usually leads to {{consequence_1}} and {{consequence_2}}. The longer it sits, the harder it gets to fix.

{{your_company_name}} helps teams like yours {{solution_statement}}. {{proof_point}}.

{{cta}}
```

**When to use in Clay:** When you have enrichment signals showing the problem exists (tech stack gaps, hiring patterns, negative reviews) but no evidence the prospect is actively shopping.

---

### BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

Best for: Prospects who need to visualize a better state before they act.

**Structure:**
1. Describe their current reality (before)
2. Paint the desired outcome (after)
3. Bridge with your offer

**Template:**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Right now, most {{job_title}}s at {{company_size}} companies are stuck {{current_state_description}}.

Imagine instead: {{desired_outcome_description}}.

That's what {{your_company_name}} helps teams achieve. {{brief_mechanism}}. {{proof_point}}.

{{cta}}
```

**When to use in Clay:** When you have growth signals (funding, hiring, expansion) that suggest the prospect is building toward a goal but hasn't optimized the path.

---

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

Best for: Pattern-interrupt openers when signals are strong.

**Structure:**
1. Attention: Lead with a specific, signal-based observation
2. Interest: Connect the observation to a relevant insight
3. Desire: Show the outcome others have achieved
4. Action: Low-friction CTA

**Template:**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

{{attention_hook_from_signal}}.

{{insight_connecting_signal_to_pain}}.

{{social_proof_or_outcome_statement}}.

{{cta}}
```

**When to use in Clay:** When you have a strong, specific signal (job change, funding round, tech adoption, public announcement) that creates a natural opening.

---

### Insight-Led (Challenger Approach)

Best for: Senior buyers who don't respond to pain-based messaging. Leads with a perspective they haven't considered.

**Structure:**
1. Share a counterintuitive insight about their situation
2. Support with data or pattern recognition
3. Connect to your offer without hard selling

**Template:**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Most {{job_title}}s assume {{common_assumption}}. But in working with {{number}} {{industry}} teams, we've found {{counterintuitive_insight}}.

The difference usually comes down to {{root_cause}}.

We put together {{resource_type}} that breaks this down. {{resource_description}}.

{{cta}}
```

**When to use in Clay:** When targeting VP+ roles where pain-based messaging feels junior. Best paired with content assets, benchmarks, or original research.

---

### Social Proof-Led

Best for: Competitive markets where credibility matters more than education.

**Structure:**
1. Reference a similar company or peer result
2. Connect the result to the prospect's likely situation
3. Offer to show how

**Template:**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

{{similar_company_name}} was dealing with {{shared_pain}} before they {{outcome_achieved}}.

Given {{company_name}} is {{signal_based_similarity}}, I thought the approach might be relevant.

{{cta}}
```

**When to use in Clay:** When you have strong case studies in the prospect's industry or company size band. Works well with lookalike company lists.

---

### Question-Led

Best for: When you want to disrupt the typical cold email pattern and prompt a mental response.

**Structure:**
1. Open with a specific, relevant question
2. Provide context for why you're asking
3. Offer value regardless of the answer

**Template:**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Has {{company_name}} started {{activity_related_to_your_solution}} yet?

Asking because {{context_for_why_you_ask}}, and most {{job_title}}s I talk to say {{common_response}}.

Either way, I put together {{resource}} that might help. {{resource_description}}.

{{cta}}
```

**When to use in Clay:** When signals are ambiguous and you need to qualify interest. The question format invites engagement even from prospects who aren't ready to buy.

## Part 2: Sequence Archetypes by Business Objective

Each archetype includes the strategic logic, recommended length, email-by-email purpose, and template structure.

---

### Archetype 1: Product-Led / Free Trial

**Objective:** Drive signups for a free trial, freemium product, or self-serve tool.

**Recommended length:** 3 emails

**Best frameworks:** PAS (Email 1), Social Proof-Led (Email 2), Question-Led (Email 3)

**Email 1 — Signal + Product Introduction**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

{{signal_based_observation}}.

{{job_title}}s focused on {{objective}} often tell us {{pain}} is the blocker. It usually shows up as {{symptom}}.

{{your_product_name}} fixes this in {{time_to_value}}. {{one_sentence_description}}. No demo required, just sign up and try it.

Would it be worth testing on {{company_name}}'s {{specific_use_case}}?
```

**Email 2 — Social Proof + Direct Link**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Quick follow-up. {{similar_company}} used {{your_product_name}} to {{specific_result}}.

Here's the link if you want to try it: {{signup_url}}

Takes about {{setup_time}} to see initial results. Would you let me know if it's useful for {{company_name}}?
```

**Email 3 — Low-Pressure Check-In**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Is {{pain}} still a priority for your team this quarter?

If so, {{your_product_name}} might be worth {{time_to_value}} of your time: {{signup_url}}

If not, no worries. Happy to reconnect when the timing is better.
```

### Archetype 2: Demo / Meeting Booking

**Objective:** Book a discovery call or product demo with a qualified prospect.

**Recommended length:** 4 emails

**Best frameworks:** AIDA (Email 1), Insight-Led (Email 2), Social Proof-Led (Email 3), Direct Ask (Email 4)

**Email 1 — Signal + Value Prop**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

{{signal_based_attention_hook}}.

{{insight_connecting_to_pain}}.

We help {{target_audience}} {{outcome_statement}}. {{proof_point}}.

Would a 15-minute walkthrough be worth your time to see if this applies to {{company_name}}?
```

**Email 2 — Reframe the Problem**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Most {{job_title}}s try to solve {{pain}} by {{common_approach}}. The issue is {{why_common_approach_fails}}.

What we've seen work instead is {{your_approach}}. {{brief_explanation}}.

Would it help to see how this looks in practice for a team like yours?
```

**Email 3 — Peer Proof**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

{{reference_customer}} had the same challenge. {{one_sentence_about_their_situation}}.

After {{your_solution_in_action}}, they {{quantified_result}}.

If {{company_name}} is dealing with something similar, I can walk you through what they did. Would that be useful?
```

**Email 4 — Direct Close or Explicit Out**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

I wanted to check one more time. Is {{outcome}} a priority for {{company_name}} right now?

If yes, here's a link to grab 15 minutes: {{booking_url}}

If not, totally understand. I'll keep you on my radar for when the timing makes sense.
```

### Archetype 3: Content / Thought Leadership

**Objective:** Build trust and stay top of mind by sharing valuable content. Softest ask, longest conversion cycle.

**Recommended length:** 3 emails

**Best frameworks:** Insight-Led (Email 1), Question-Led (Email 2), BAB (Email 3)

**Email 1 — Share Original Insight**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

We just published {{content_type}} on {{topic}}: {{content_url}}

The core finding: {{key_insight}}.

I thought it'd be relevant given {{company_name}} is {{signal_or_context}}.

Would you find it useful if I flagged similar research when we publish it?
```

**Email 2 — Engage on Their Perspective**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Curious about your take on something.

{{question_related_to_content_topic}}?

We've been hearing different answers from {{job_title}}s depending on {{variable}}, and I'd be interested in the {{company_name}} perspective.

Either way, here's the piece I mentioned last time in case you missed it: {{content_url}}
```

**Email 3 — Invite to Contribute**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

We're putting together {{content_project}} featuring {{job_title}}s from {{industry}} companies.

Given your work at {{company_name}} on {{relevant_area}}, I thought you'd have a strong perspective to share.

Would you be open to a short conversation for it? Usually takes about {{time_commitment}}.
```

### Archetype 4: Event / Webinar Invitation

**Objective:** Drive registrations for a live event, webinar, or virtual session.

**Recommended length:** 3 emails

**Best frameworks:** AIDA (Email 1), Social Proof-Led (Email 2), Direct Ask (Email 3)

**Email 1 — Announce + Relevance**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

We're hosting {{event_name}} on {{event_date}}. It covers {{topic}} with {{speakers_or_format}}.

Given {{company_name}} is {{signal_or_context}}, I thought this might be directly relevant.

Details and registration: {{event_url}}

Would this be useful for your team?
```

**Email 2 — Social Proof + Urgency**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Quick follow-up on {{event_name}}. We've already got {{job_title}}s from {{notable_companies}} registered.

The session covers {{specific_takeaway_1}} and {{specific_takeaway_2}}.

Here's the registration link: {{event_url}}

Would it be worth {{time_commitment}} of your time?
```

**Email 3 — Final Reminder**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Last note on this. {{event_name}} is {{time_until_event}}.

If {{pain_or_topic}} is something your team is working through, this session will give you {{specific_value}}.

Register here: {{event_url}}
```

### Archetype 5: Partnership / Co-Marketing

**Objective:** Initiate partnerships, integrations, co-marketing, or channel relationships.

**Recommended length:** 3 emails

**Best frameworks:** BAB (Email 1), Social Proof-Led (Email 2), Direct Ask (Email 3)

**Email 1 — Shared Audience, Mutual Value**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

{{company_name}} and {{your_company_name}} serve a similar audience: {{shared_audience_description}}.

We've been exploring ways to create more value for {{audience_type}} by partnering with companies like yours. {{partnership_type_description}}.

Would you be open to a quick conversation about whether there's a fit?
```

**Email 2 — Example of What's Working**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

We recently partnered with {{partner_example}} on {{partnership_description}}. The result: {{quantified_outcome}}.

I think there's a similar opportunity with {{company_name}} given {{overlap_or_signal}}.

Would it help to share what the {{partner_example}} collaboration looked like so you can evaluate fit?
```

**Email 3 — Simple Next Step**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Wanted to check if partnership opportunities are something {{company_name}} is exploring right now.

If so, I can share a one-pager on what we've done with similar companies and how it's worked.

If the timing isn't right, no pressure at all.
```

### Archetype 6: Re-Engagement (Dormant / Churned)

**Objective:** Reactivate past prospects who went cold or customers who churned.

**Recommended length:** 3 emails

**Best frameworks:** BAB (Email 1), Social Proof-Led (Email 2), Question-Led (Email 3)

**Email 1 — Acknowledge + New Value**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

We spoke {{timeframe}} ago about {{original_topic}}. I know the timing wasn't right then.

Since then, we've {{new_development_1}} and {{new_development_2}}.

Given {{recent_signal_about_company}}, I thought it might be worth reconnecting.

Would you be open to a quick catch-up?
```

**Email 2 — What's Changed**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Quick follow-up. Since we last connected, {{customer_example}} started using {{your_product}} and {{result_achieved}}.

The biggest change on our side is {{key_improvement}}. It directly addresses {{original_objection_or_gap}}.

Would it be worth a fresh look?
```

**Email 3 — Final Check**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Is {{original_pain}} still something {{company_name}} is working on?

If yes, I'd love to show you what's new. If priorities have shifted, I'm happy to take this off your plate.

Either way, just let me know.
```

### Archetype 7: Referral / Warm Introduction Request

**Objective:** Ask existing contacts for introductions to other prospects.

**Recommended length:** 2 emails

**Best frameworks:** Direct Ask (Email 1), Follow-Up (Email 2)

**Email 1 — Context + Ask**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Hope things are going well at {{company_name}}.

I'm looking to connect with more {{target_role}}s at {{target_company_type}} companies. Given your network, I thought you might know someone who'd benefit from {{value_statement}}.

Would you be open to making an introduction? Happy to send a short blurb you can forward.
```

**Email 2 — Make It Easy**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Following up on my last note. To make this easy, here's a short blurb you can forward to anyone you think might benefit:

"{{forwardable_blurb}}"

No pressure at all. If anyone comes to mind, I'd appreciate the intro.
```

### Archetype 8: Upsell / Cross-Sell (Existing Customers)

**Objective:** Expand usage or introduce new products to current customers.

**Recommended length:** 3 emails

**Best frameworks:** BAB (Email 1), Social Proof-Led (Email 2), Direct Ask (Email 3)

**Email 1 — Connect Current Usage to New Value**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

I noticed {{company_name}} has been {{current_usage_pattern}} with {{current_product}}.

Teams that reach this stage often benefit from {{new_product_or_feature}} because {{reason}}.

Would it be worth a quick look at how this could add to what you're already doing?
```

**Email 2 — Peer Expansion Story**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

{{similar_customer}} started the same way. After {{timeframe}} on {{current_product}}, they added {{new_product}} and {{quantified_result}}.

Given where {{company_name}} is now, I think the trajectory is similar.

Would it help to see how they set it up?
```

**Email 3 — Direct Offer**

```
{Hi|Hey|Hello} {{first_name}},

Since {{company_name}} is already getting value from {{current_product}}, I wanted to offer {{specific_offer}} on {{new_product}}.

If expanding is something you're considering, I can walk you through it in {{time_commitment}}: {{booking_url}}

If not, no worries. Just wanted to make sure you knew the option was there.
```

## Part 3: Hook & Opener Patterns

These are interchangeable openers for Email 1. Mix and match with any framework above.

### Signal-Based Hooks

Use when Clay enrichment provides a specific, recent data point.

| Hook Type | Template | Best Signal Source |
|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| Observation | "I noticed {{company_name}} just {{specific_action}}." | Funding, hiring, tech adoption |
| Congratulation | "Congrats on {{achievement}}. That usually means {{implication}}." | Funding rounds, awards, launches |
| News Reference | "Saw the news about {{company_name}} and {{event}}." | Press mentions, product launches |
| Job Posting | "I saw {{company_name}} is hiring for {{role}}. That often signals {{implication}}." | Job board data |
| Tech Stack | "With {{detected_tool}} in your stack, you've probably run into {{common_challenge}}." | BuiltWith, Wappalyzer |

### Pattern Interrupt Hooks

Use when signals are weak but you want to stand out.

| Hook Type | Template |
|-----------|----------|
| Contrarian | "Most {{job_title}}s think {{common_belief}}. The data says otherwise." |
| Statistic | "{{percentage}}% of {{target_group}} report {{finding}}. {{company_name}} might be in that bucket." |
| Confession | "I'll be honest, I almost didn't reach out. But then I saw {{signal}} and changed my mind." |
| Prediction | "Based on {{signal}}, I'd predict {{company_name}} will face {{challenge}} in the next {{timeframe}}." |

### Relationship-Based Hooks

Use when there's a warm path (shared connections, past interaction, community).

| Hook Type | Template |
|-----------|----------|
| Mutual Connection | "{{connection_name}} suggested I reach out. They mentioned {{context}}." |
| Shared Community | "We're both in {{community_name}}. Your post on {{topic}} caught my attention." |
| Past Interaction | "We {{interaction_type}} at {{event_or_context}}. Following up on {{topic}}." |
| Content Engagement | "I saw your {{post_type}} on {{topic}}. {{specific_reaction}}." |

## Part 4: CTA Patterns by Sequence Stage

CTAs should escalate in directness as the sequence progresses. Never start with a meeting ask.

### Email 1 CTAs (Lowest Friction)

| Type | Template |
|------|----------|
| Value Offer | "Would you like me to run a free {{audit_type}} for {{company_name}}?" |
| Resource Share | "Want me to send over the {{resource}} that covers this?" |
| Interest Check | "Is this something your team is thinking about right now?" |
| Permission | "Would it be helpful if I shared how {{similar_company}} handled this?" |
| Open Question | "How are you currently approaching {{challenge}}?" |

### Email 2 CTAs (Low Friction)

| Type | Template |
|------|----------|
| Feedback | "Would you let me know if this is useful for {{company_name}}?" |
| Direct Link | "Here's the link if you want to try it: {{url}}" |
| Comparison | "Curious how this compares to what you're doing today?" |
| Nudge | "Worth testing? Takes about {{time}} to see results." |

### Email 3 CTAs (Medium Friction)

| Type | Template |
|------|----------|
| Conversation | "Would a short conversation be worth your time to explore this?" |
| Walkthrough | "Want me to walk you through how {{similar_company}} set this up?" |
| Contribute | "Would you be open to sharing your perspective for {{content_project}}?" |
| Specific Ask | "Would {{day}} or {{day}} work for a 15-minute call?" |

### Email 4 CTAs (Direct, With Exit)

| Type | Template |
|------|----------|
| Priority Check | "Is {{outcome}} a priority for {{company_name}} right now?" |
| Booking Link | "If it's worth a conversation: {{booking_url}}" |
| Explicit Out | "If not, no worries at all. I'll keep you on my radar." |
| Binary | "Should I keep you in the loop on this, or take you off my list?" |

## Part 5: Subject Line Formulas

All subject lines should be 2-5 words, lowercase, and focused on pain, outcome, or curiosity.

### By Category

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| Pain-focused | "proving roi", "pipeline leaks", "attribution gaps", "churn problem" |
| Outcome-focused | "faster pipeline", "lower cac", "better conversion", "revenue per visitor" |
| Observation | "noticed something", "saw your post", "re: {{company_name}}", "your {{tool}} setup" |
| Question | "quick question", "curious about this", "is this a priority" |
| Trigger-based | "congrats on funding", "re: new hire", "saw the announcement" |
| Curiosity | "thought you'd want this", "something for {{company_name}}", "found this useful" |
| Contrarian | "this doesn't work", "unpopular take", "most teams get this wrong" |
| Time-sensitive | "before q2 planning", "before the freeze", "end of quarter" |

### Rules

- Never use title case or ALL CAPS
- Never use exclamation marks
- Never use spam trigger words (free, guaranteed, act now, limited time)
- Subject lines on follow-up emails should be blank (reply in same thread)
- Test 3-5 variations per campaign and let data decide

## Part 6: Conditional Logic Patterns for Clay

Every template above assumes all enrichment data is available. In practice, data is often missing. These patterns handle gaps.

### Required vs. Optional Fields

| Field Type | Examples | If Missing |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| Required | first_name, company_name, job_title | Do not send. Filter row out. |
| High-value | signal data, tech stack, funding | Fall back to pain-first framework |
| Nice-to-have | employee count, industry, revenue | Use generic phrasing |

### Fallback Framework Selection

```
IF signal_data exists → Use AIDA or Signal-Based Hook
ELSE IF tech_stack data exists → Use PAS with stack reference
ELSE IF industry + job_title exist → Use Insight-Led
ELSE → Use Pain-First PAS (most generic, still effective)
```

### Missing Data Phrasing

| Missing Field | Instead Of | Write |
|---------------|-----------|-------|
| detected_tools | "I noticed you're using Optimizely" | "Teams in your space often rely on testing tools" |
| funding_amount | "Congrats on the $10M Series A" | "Given {{company_name}}'s growth trajectory" |
| employee_count | "With a team of 200+" | "At your stage" |
| specific_pain | "Attribution gaps in your funnel" | "The challenge most {{job_title}}s mention first" |

## Part 7: Selecting the Right Archetype

Use this decision tree when building a new Clay outbound table.

```
What is the primary goal?
│
├─ Drive product signups → Archetype 1: Product-Led / Free Trial
│
├─ Book meetings / demos → Archetype 2: Demo / Meeting Booking
│
├─ Build awareness / trust → Archetype 3: Content / Thought Leadership
│
├─ Fill event seats → Archetype 4: Event / Webinar Invitation
│
├─ Initiate partnerships → Archetype 5: Partnership / Co-Marketing
│
├─ Reactivate cold leads → Archetype 6: Re-Engagement
│
├─ Get introductions → Archetype 7: Referral / Warm Introduction
│
├─ Expand existing accounts → Archetype 8: Upsell / Cross-Sell
│
├─ Promote free tools + content → See: 3 Email Sequence reference
│
└─ Sell workshops / services → See: 4 Email Sequence reference
```

## Part 8: Adapting Templates for Clay Implementation

### Step 1: Define your archetype and framework

Choose from Part 2 (archetype) and Part 1 (framework). These determine structure.

### Step 2: Map your enrichment signals to hooks

Use Part 3 to select the right opener based on what data Clay provides.

### Step 3: Select CTAs by email position

Use Part 4 to escalate friction appropriately through the sequence.

### Step 4: Write subject lines

Use Part 5 formulas. Generate 3-5 variations for testing.

### Step 5: Implement conditional logic

Use Part 6 to handle missing data. Every email must render cleanly even with gaps.

### Step 6: Build modular components in Clay

Do not generate entire emails in a single AI call. Break into:
- Hook/opener (1 AI column)
- Pain/insight bridge (1 AI column)
- Solution/proof (1 AI column)
- CTA (1 formula or AI column)
- Final assembly (1 merge column)

This modular approach scores highest on the Clay Automated Outbound Certification rubric and allows testing individual components.

### Step 7: QA before export

- Preview 10+ rows to verify personalization depth
- Check all conditional logic paths (with and without data)
- Verify plain-text formatting (no markdown, no HTML unless targeting Lemlist HTML)
- Confirm all links are full plain-text URLs
- Ensure no placeholders leak into final output
