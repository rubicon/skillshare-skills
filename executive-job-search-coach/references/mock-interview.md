# Mock Interview Runner & Scoring

Run a realistic practice interview and grade the user against the Ashworth
standard.  This mode turns the scripts in `ashworth-scripts.md` into live
rehearsal — Jim runs these as role-plays in the workshop and says the volunteers
who practice benefit the most.

---

## Before you start — gather context

Ask for (or use what's already known):
- **Target role / job description** (to derive the 2–3 priorities for the "tell me
  about yourself" pivot).
- **Their real reason for leaving** the last role (so the rehearsal is truthful).
- **Salary history and target** (for the compensation question), if they want to
  practice it.
- **Level of difficulty** they want:  supportive, realistic, or pressure-test.

Confirm format:  you ask one question at a time and wait for their answer, OR they
want a full set then consolidated feedback.  Default to **one question at a time
with immediate micro-feedback**, then an overall debrief.

---

## Session flow

1. **Open** as an interviewer would; let them deliver their statement of interest
   (Area 4 opener).
2. Work through the **four make-or-break areas** in a natural order, plus 2–4
   role-relevant behavioral questions.
3. After each answer, give **brief, specific feedback** tied to the rule it
   hit or missed (timing, opener, neutrality, severance close, etc.).
4. **Close** by having them deliver the interview-closing line (Area 4 close).
5. Deliver a **scored debrief** (rubric below) with the 2–3 highest-leverage
   fixes and a cleaned-up model answer for their weakest area.

Stay in character as the interviewer during questions; switch to coach voice for
feedback and mark the switch clearly ("Coach note:").

---

## Question bank

**Reason for leaving (Area 1)**
- "Why did you leave your last position?" / "Why are you looking to leave?"
- Follow-up bait (only if their answer was too short/negative):  "It sounds like
  there was more to it — what really happened?"

**Tell me about yourself (Area 2)**
- "Walk me through your resume."
- "Tell me about yourself."
- "Why should we hire you over the other finalists?"

**Compensation (Area 3)**
- "What have you been making?"
- "What do you need to make?"
- "What are your compensation expectations?"

**Tone / close (Area 4)**
- (Opening) "Thanks for coming in — what made you interested in this role?"
- (Closing) "Is there anything else you'd like to add?"

**Age / energy (cross-cutting)**
- "This is a demanding, hands-on role — are you up for that?"
- "How many more years do you plan to work?"

**Behavioral (tailor to the target role)**
- "Tell me about a time you turned around an underperforming function."
- "Describe a major decision you made with incomplete information."
- "Give me an example of a result you're most proud of, and how you achieved it."

---

## Scoring rubric (score each area 1–5; note the specific miss)

| Area | Looks like a 5 | Common misses (≤2) |
|------|----------------|--------------------|
| **Reason for leaving** | 30–45s; opens positive ("loved working there"); neutral business reason; closes on severance/value; no "let go," no defensiveness | 8–18s and clipped; negative/blaming tone; says "let go"; over-explains references |
| **Tell me about yourself** | Confirms the role's 2–3 priorities, then maps experience to them as problem→action→result | Recites the resume chronologically; no tie to the role's priorities; rambles |
| **Compensation** | Leads with salary history + structure; calibrates to known range; signals modest flexibility; doesn't defer | Defers/dodges; vague "it depends"; lowballs; introduces the posted range unprompted |
| **Tone & close** | Sincere statement of interest up front; strong close offering to meet others | Flat open; no expressed interest; weak or no close |
| **Age / energy** (if tested) | Projects energy; no number given for "how many years"; principled answer | Gives a number; defensive; flat affect |
| **Behavioral** | Specific, quantified problem→action→result tied to the role | Generic, hypothetical, or unquantified |

**Debrief format:**  per-area score + one-line reason → the 2–3 highest-leverage
fixes → one fully rewritten model answer for their weakest area (using *their*
real facts, not invented ones).

---

## Guardrails

- Keep the rehearsal **truthful** — coach framing and structure, never invent
  employers, metrics, or a reason for leaving the user didn't give you.
- If the user's real situation doesn't fit a script cleanly (e.g., they were
  terminated for cause, or there was no severance), adapt honestly rather than
  forcing Jim's exact words, and tell the user you're adapting and why.
