# Ashworth Resume Standard — Do's, Don'ts, model layout, review modes

Source:  Jim Ashworth, "Resumes Do's and Don'ts," Ashworth Tomerlin & Associates.
This is the standard the resume-review mode enforces.

---

## Two review sub-modes — ask the user which one

Always confirm which sub-mode the user wants before reviewing.  Default to
**Advisory** if unspecified.

### Advisory review (default)

Critique the resume against the rules below.  For each issue:  name the rule,
quote or point to the offending text, explain *why* Jim flags it, and recommend a
fix.  Leave final editorial judgment to the user.  Summarize with a prioritized
list (highest-impact fixes first) and an overall read on two-page balance and eye
appeal.

A summary/profile paragraph is allowed in this mode, provided it obeys the
three-consecutive-line rule (≤3 lines).  If an existing summary runs longer,
recommend compressing it to ≤3 lines rather than deleting it.

### Strict Conform

**Force the resume to match Jim's Do's & Don'ts and model format exactly.**  Treat
every rule below as non-negotiable and rewrite the resume to conform.  Do not
soften or skip a rule because the user's existing resume departs from it — the
point of this sub-mode is exact conformance.  In particular, remove the prose
summary/profile paragraph entirely — per Jim's model resume, the "Summary of
Qualifications" is the keyword grid only, with no prose summary paragraph.  Where
conformance needs a fact you
don't have (a company revenue line, a clean single title, a graduation year),
insert a clearly-marked `[FILL IN]` placeholder rather than inventing it.  After
rewriting, run the checklist at the bottom and report any rule you could not fully
satisfy and why.

---

## What TO do (verbatim rules)

1. **Balance the resume on two pages.**
2. **Make the first column and the first half of the second column of your
   "Summary of Qualifications" the things you most want to do or are best at.**
   (This grid is what the reader sees first — load it with the target, not the
   incidental.)
3. **Provide a one- to two-line, italicized description of each company you worked
   for.**
4. **Leave months off your dates** — nobody cares and months are a distraction.
   Use years only.
5. **Print your original resume and this format side by side and show your spouse
   (or an unbiased reader)** to judge which has more eye appeal and is more
   readable.

## What NOT to do (verbatim rules)

1. **Don't use the word "Executive."**  It worries employers that you won't roll
   up your sleeves.
2. **No narrative paragraphs.**  Narrative formats don't get read.  Rule of thumb
   from print media:  a **maximum of three consecutive lines.**  (Jim's example of
   a bad "Executive Summary" ran seven lines.)
3. **Don't stack multiple titles at the top of the resume** (e.g.,
   "CFO/Controller/VP of Finance").  It makes the employer think you're either
   over- or under-qualified.  **One title per role.**
4. **Show graduation dates.**  Leaving them off is a dead giveaway you're trying
   to hide your age — and prompts the reader to guess older than reality.

### Summary / profile paragraph

How a summary/profile paragraph is treated depends on the review sub-mode, and it
turns on the three-consecutive-line rule above (What NOT to do #2):

- **Advisory:**  a summary/profile paragraph is permitted, provided it stays
  within the three-consecutive-line rule (≤3 lines).  If an existing summary runs
  longer, recommend compressing it to ≤3 lines rather than deleting it.
- **Strict Conform:**  remove the prose summary/profile paragraph entirely.  Per
  Jim's model resume, the "Summary of Qualifications" is the keyword grid only —
  there is no prose summary paragraph.

## Model resume layout (Jim's example)

Use this as the structural target for Strict Conform and as the visual reference
for Advisory.

```
                         Name, [Credential e.g. CPA]
                         City, State (optional)
                         Phone Number
                         Email
                         LinkedIn  ← hyperlinked

                    SUMMARY OF QUALIFICATIONS
   (two/three-column keyword grid; col 1 + first half of col 2 = what you
    most want to do / are best at)
   Mergers & Acquisitions     Private Equity        Team Development
   Treasury Management        Multi-Unit/Location   Acquisition Integration
   Budgeting & Forecasting    Process Improvement   Contract Negotiations
   Strategic/Capital Planning ERP Implementation    SEC Reporting
   ...

                    PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE
   Company – City, ST                                       2016 – present
   (italic) One- to two-line description of the company:  what it does,
   revenue, employee count, locations.
   Title
   • Achievement bullet (problem → action → result), ≤ 3 lines
   • Achievement bullet
   ...

   Company – City, ST                                       2008 – 2016
   (italic) Company description.
   Senior Title (2014 – 2016)
   • ...
   Earlier Title (2008 – 2014)
   • ...

                    EDUCATION AND CERTIFICATION
   Master of Business Administration   2008   ← keep the year
   [School]
   BSBA – Accounting   2005   ← keep the year
   [School]
   Certified Public Accountant – [State], License #
```

Notes drawn from the model:
- Dates right-aligned, **years only**.
- Each role gets an italic company descriptor line.
- When a person held two titles at one company, show them as **dated sub-roles**
  under that company — this is *not* the same as the forbidden "multiple titles
  stacked at the top of the resume."
- Bullets are achievement-oriented and obey the three-line cap.

## Strict Conform checklist (run after rewriting)

- [ ] Resume balances on **two pages**.
- [ ] "Summary of Qualifications" grid leads with target competencies (col 1 +
      first half of col 2).
- [ ] **No prose summary/profile paragraph** — keyword grid only.
- [ ] Every company has a **one- to two-line italic descriptor**.
- [ ] All dates are **years only** (no months).
- [ ] **Graduation dates present** with each degree.
- [ ] The word **"Executive" removed** wherever it appeared as a self-label.
- [ ] **One title per role** at the top; multi-title stacks eliminated (sub-roles
      under a single company are fine, dated).
- [ ] **No narrative block exceeds three consecutive lines.**
- [ ] **LinkedIn hyperlink** present in the header.
- [ ] Any missing facts marked `[FILL IN]`, not invented.
